Personalized Medical Recommendation System Using Explainable AI

Developing a machine learning-based system that leverages Explainable AI techniques including SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) and LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) to provide transparent and personalized medical recommendations. The system aims to bridge the gap between complex ML models and clinical interpretability, enabling healthcare professionals to understand and trust AI-driven decisions.

The research focuses on building models that not only achieve high predictive accuracy but also provide human-understandable explanations for each recommendation, which is critical in the medical domain.

Emotion Detection from Facial Images

Working on a deep learning model to detect and classify human emotions from facial images using computer vision techniques. The system uses Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) trained on facial expression datasets to identify emotions such as happiness, sadness, anger, surprise, fear, disgust, and neutral states.

The research explores various model architectures and data augmentation strategies to improve accuracy and robustness across different lighting conditions, facial orientations, and demographic groups.
